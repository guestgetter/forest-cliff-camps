Safety & Accreditation Most read

Accreditation

Since 1997, Forest Cliff has been accredited by the Ontario Camps Association (OCA). We're also members of Christian Camping International Canada. Inflatable amusement devices are licensed with TSSA.

Equipment & Activity Safety

Every activity area has 1–2 highly trained staff who directly observe operations, conduct debriefs, and pull worn equipment from service. All ropes, carabiners, and boats are inspected before each use.

  • High adventure — inspected annually by Adventureworks (independent, certified)
  • Inflatables — inspected by TSSA; maintained by certified ADM-I mechanics
  • Archery — maintained through The Archer's Nook
  • Waterfront — supervised by NLS-certified lifeguards
  • Boats/PWC — serviced by certified marine technicians

Personal Safety

No staff member is ever alone in a private location with a camper. All visitors sign in and wear ID badges. We follow the Duty to Report under the Child and Family Services Act.

Staff-to-camper ratios: Overnight Camp is 1:5. Day Camp is 1:7. These exceed accreditation requirements. Campers who need 1:1 support can register for additional assistance.

Emergency Preparedness

All staff are trained in CPR, First Aid, and our Emergency Action Plan covering severe weather, fire, and medical emergencies. A signal system allows instant communication across all sites. Lead staff are connected via radios and phones. Overnight Camp has a health care team available 24/7.

Staff & Training

Who we hire

We only hire the best. Interviews cover faith maturity, experience with kids, and overall competencies. We require 3 positive, non-family references and a vulnerable sector check for anyone directly responsible for children.

Why paid staff? While many camps use volunteers, we've found that hiring staff ensures consistent, quality experiences all summer long. We attract the best Christian young adults by compensating them fairly.

Training areas

  • Camper Care — behaviour, bullying, homesickness, bedwetting, individual attention
  • Safety Protocols — severe weather, fire, medical emergencies
  • Child Protection — prevention and reporting of suspected abuse
  • Conflict Management — helping campers work through disagreements
  • Guest Services — greeting families, creating an exceptional experience
  • First Aid — bee stings, stomach aches, medication distribution
  • Suicide Alertness — recognizing and responding to at-risk youth
  • The Gospel — sharing faith in a truthful, sensitive, age-appropriate way

Activity staff receive additional training specific to their area, with emphasis on safety and engaging all campers. Lifeguards hold NLS certification. High adventure staff are trained through Adventureworks. Boating staff hold PCOC and driver's licenses.

Registration & Payment

How to register

Only a deposit is required to hold your spot:

  • $50 per Day Camp session
  • $200 per Overnight Camp session

Balances are due May 1. Payment plans are available.

Payment methods

  • E-transfer (preferred — lower processing fees) — send to info@forestcliff.ca, no password required
  • Pre-authorized debit — enter banking info during registration (3–5 business days to process)
  • Visa & MasterCard — accepted, but other methods help keep fees low

Friend requests

Request one cabin mate (Overnight, same gender/similar age) or one group mate (Day Camp, similar age) during registration. Email info@forestcliff.ca for additional requests or sibling arrangements.

Cancellation policy

  • 45+ days before camp — full refund (less $40 admin fee)
  • 8–44 days before — 50% refund (less $40 admin fee)
  • 7 days or less — non-refundable

Refunds processed within 3 weeks. To cancel or transfer: info@forestcliff.ca or 519-913-2744 x 100.

Financial Assistance

Family discount

  • Day Camp — $30 off the 3rd+ child from the same household
  • Overnight Camp — $50 off the 3rd+ child for Kids Camp, Jr. High, or SALT

Bursaries

Income-based bursaries cover a percentage of one full week of Overnight Camp. During registration, select "bursary" when asked about fee coverage. The bursary deposit is $50/camper (fully refundable if not approved). Send your most recent Notice of Assessment to info@forestcliff.ca.

SummerFund

A referral-based program allowing organizations to request subsidized registration for a child in their care community. Made possible by generous donors.

Volunteer discount

Parents/guardians who volunteer the same week receive 50% off their camper's registration. Roles include housekeeping, maintenance, cook's helper, office, and Health Centre. Babysitting for younger children can be arranged.

  • Junior Camp (Sun–Tue) — Sunday 4:00 PM
  • Junior Camp (Wed–Fri) — Wednesday 2:00 PM

Parent dinner

For parents of Kids Campers and Junior Campers (Sunday start) — once your camper is settled, join us for dinner at 5:00 PM and stay for the Opening Rally at 6:00 PM. Siblings welcome.

Health & medical

All campers and staff wear t-shirts at the waterfront (sun safety). Send a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, bug spray, and a water bottle. Our kitchen handles special diets and food allergies (additional charge may apply for vegetarian, dairy-free, egg-free, or gluten-free).

Peanut/nut free property. No peanut or tree nut products may be brought to camp.

All medications go to the Health Centre at check-in (original packaging). EpiPens and inhalers can stay with the camper if you choose — discuss at the Health Centre.

Packing list

  • Pajamas, underwear, extra socks
  • Warm jacket or sweatshirt
  • Hat and raincoat/poncho
  • Swimsuit and t-shirt for swimming
  • Beach towel and sunscreen
  • Swim shoes/sandals (can get wet)
  • Closed-toed shoes (required)
  • Towel, washcloth, toiletries
  • Sleeping bag and pillow (bagged)
  • Water bottle
  • Shorts, t-shirts, long pants
  • Insect repellent
  • Disposable camera (optional)
  • Medication in original package
  • Plastic bag for dirty/wet laundry
  • Twin sheet for mattress (optional)
Do NOT pack: Cell phones, smart watches, gaming devices, spending money, weapons, lighters, tobacco/vapes, peanut/nut products, over-the-counter medication (supplied by Health Centre).

Mail & care packages

Campers love getting mail! Leave notes at the check-in table, or email mail@forestcliff.ca with the camper's name and cabin number in the subject line. Emails received by 11:00 AM are printed and delivered at lunch.

Locations

Various church locations across Southwestern Ontario, plus a new Day Camp at the Lake session in 2026 at the Lambton Shores property.

Daily schedule

  • Check-in: 8:45 AM (8:15 AM at the Lake; 8:30 AM for ACT)
  • Pick-up: 4:00 PM daily — photo ID required, no exceptions
  • Extended care: Until 5:15 PM — $45/camper/week (flat rate)

What to bring

  • Hat
  • Sunscreen
  • Water bottle
  • Closed-toed shoes
  • Water shoes (for water days)
  • Bathing suit
  • Extra t-shirt (water activities)
  • Towel
  • Plastic bag for wet items
  • Lunch and snacks (peanut/nut safe)
  • Medication if needed (original packaging)

Health & safety

Complete first aid kits at every location. Sunscreen applied every 2 hours. Water breaks every 15 minutes on extreme heat days. We serve only peanut/tree nut-free snacks. Parents must send peanut/nut-safe lunches.

Support for complex needs

Day Camp doesn't provide 1:1 support, but we collaborate with families to accommodate existing support workers. Email support@forestcliff.ca if your child has complex needs.

Support for Campers with Complex Needs

Forest Cliff wants every camper to have an amazing experience. Through our integrative programming, we strive to meet complex needs — physical, behavioural, dietary, or medical.

Overnight Camp

Campers with complex needs may be paired with a 1:1 cabin leader for support and individual attention. We'll collect additional information to determine how to create the best experience. Contact support@forestcliff.ca.

Day Camp & Day Camp at the Lake

We don't provide 1:1 support at Day Camp, but we welcome families to bring their existing support worker (EA, respite worker, etc.). Email support@forestcliff.ca to coordinate.

Tip: Even if your child doesn't need 1:1 support, include any helpful details in the online registration — group leaders use this info to give your camper the best possible week.
Behaviour Management & Anti-Bullying

All group leaders are trained in behaviour management and anti-bullying. Our approach:

  • Remove the child from the situation privately
  • Partner with the camper to find a solution that works for everyone
  • Grace is given; consequences are enforced when necessary
  • If behaviour doesn't change, parents/guardians are called to discuss options

The directors reserve the right to dismiss any child who has rejected the reasonable controls of camp.
